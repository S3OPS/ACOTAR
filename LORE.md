# ACOTAR Fantasy RPG - Lore Reference

## Overview
This document provides lore-accurate information from the ACOTAR (A Court of Thorns and Roses) series by Sarah J. Maas. This game faithfully recreates the world of Prythian.

## The World of Prythian

### The Seven Courts
Prythian is divided into seven courts, each ruled by a High Lord with unique powers:

#### 1. **Spring Court**
- **High Lord**: Tamlin (as of Book 1-2)
- **Powers**: Shapeshifting, nature manipulation, life force, animal transformation
- **Description**: Land of eternal spring with blooming gardens and temperate weather. Known for shapeshifters.
- **Key Locations**: Spring Court Manor, Tamlin's Estate
- **Symbol**: Green and gold
- **Notable Members**: Tamlin (High Lord), Lucien (former emissary), Alis (servant)

#### 2. **Night Court**
- **High Lord**: Rhysand
- **High Lady**: Feyre Archeron (first High Lady in Prythian history)
- **Powers**: Darkness manipulation, mind reading (Daemati), winnowing, mist and shadow control
- **Description**: Split between the Court of Nightmares (Hewn City) and Velaris (City of Starlight). Most powerful court in Prythian.
- **Key Locations**: 
  - Velaris - The hidden, beautiful City of Starlight
  - Hewn City - The Court of Nightmares
  - Illyrian Mountains - Warrior training camps
  - House of Wind - Mountain fortress with vast library
  - The Prison - Ancient prison for dangerous beings
  - The Rainbow - Artist quarter in Velaris
  - Rita's - Famous bar in Velaris
- **Symbol**: Black and silver with stars
- **Inner Circle**: Rhysand, Feyre, Cassian, Azriel, Mor, Amren

#### 3. **Summer Court**
- **High Lord**: Tarquin (youngest High Lord in history)
- **Powers**: Water manipulation, ocean control
- **Description**: Coastal realm with warm waters and pristine beaches. Known for progressive policies.
- **Key Locations**: Adriata (capital city), underwater palaces
- **Symbol**: Blue and turquoise
- **Notable Members**: Tarquin, Cresseida, Varian

#### 4. **Autumn Court**
- **High Lord**: Beron (cruel ruler)
- **Powers**: Fire manipulation, seasonal magic
- **Description**: Land of eternal autumn with forests of red, gold, and orange. Known for political scheming.
- **Symbol**: Red, orange, and brown
- **Notable Members**: Beron (High Lord), Lady of Autumn, Eris (heir), Lucien (exiled son)

#### 5. **Winter Court**
- **High Lord**: Kallias
- **High Lady**: Viviane (his mate and co-ruler)
- **Powers**: Ice and snow manipulation, winter magic
- **Description**: Frozen realm of eternal winter. Known for their fierce loyalty.
- **Symbol**: White and ice blue
- **Notable Members**: Kallias, Viviane

#### 6. **Dawn Court**
- **High Lord**: Thesan
- **Powers**: Healing magic, light manipulation, sunrise powers
- **Description**: Land of perpetual sunrise, known for the greatest healers in Prythian
- **Symbol**: Pink, gold, and peach
- **Notable Members**: Thesan, his Peregryn lover

#### 7. **Day Court**
- **High Lord**: Helion Spell-Cleaver
- **Powers**: Light manipulation, spell-breaking, ancient magic
- **Description**: Sun-drenched realm, home to scholars and the greatest libraries in Prythian
- **Symbol**: Gold and white
- **Notable Members**: Helion (secretly Lucien's biological father)

### The Wall
- Magical barrier created by the Treaty after the War
- Separates the mortal lands from Prythian
- Can only be crossed by the Fae or with their permission
- **BROKEN** by the King of Hybern during events of ACOMAF/ACOWAR

### Other Realms
- **Hybern**: Island kingdom ruled by the King of Hybern, separated from Prythian by the sea
- **The Middle**: Treacherous neutral territory where the Weaver dwells
- **Under the Mountain**: Amarantha's domain during her 49-year curse

## Character Classes

### High Fae
- Immortal, powerful magical beings
- Can possess various magical abilities
- Immune to human diseases and aging
- Enhanced strength, speed, and senses
- Can be born High Fae or Made by the Cauldron
- Examples: Rhysand, Tamlin, Mor, Amarantha

### Lesser Fae
- Magical beings of lesser power than High Fae
- Varied forms and abilities
- Include creatures like:
  - Naga (serpent creatures)
  - Bogge (shapeshifting creatures)
  - Water wraiths
  - Puca (shapeshifters)
  - Various other Fae creatures

### Humans
- Mortal beings without magic (unless Made)
- Live south of the Wall
- Can be transformed into High Fae by the Cauldron
- Notable humans turned Fae: Feyre, Nesta, Elain Archeron

### Illyrians
- Warrior race from the Night Court mountains
- Possess powerful membranous wings (bat-like)
- Expert fighters and warriors
- Live in war camps with strict hierarchy
- Practice wing-clipping on females (controversial tradition)
- Notable members: Cassian (General), Azriel (Shadowsinger), Rhysand (half-Illyrian)

### Special Creatures

#### Suriel
- Ancient Fae creatures with skeletal appearance
- Bound to tell the truth when captured
- Can be trapped with ash wood circles
- Possess vast knowledge of past, present, and potential futures
- Feyre captures one in Book 1

#### Attor
- Winged creature servants with reptilian features
- Served Amarantha and Hybern
- Swift and deadly in combat
- Known for cruelty

## Magic System

### Winnowing
- Teleportation ability
- Requires great power and focus
- Can transport self and others
- Some Fae can winnow across vast distances

### Daemati
- Mind reading and manipulation
- Extremely rare ability
- Can enter, read, and control minds
- Can communicate telepathically through mental pathways
- Notable Daemati: Rhysand, Feyre

### Shadowsinger
- Unique ability possessed only by Azriel
- Shadows whisper secrets and information
- Can meld with and control shadows
- Different from simple darkness manipulation

### TruthTelling
- Mor's unique ability
- Power to detect lies and compel truth
- Raw, devastating magical force
- Origin of her title "The Morrigan"

### Shapeshifting
- Ability to change physical form
- Spring Court specialty (Tamlin)
- Day Court also possesses this (Helion)
- Requires practice and significant power

### Elemental Magic
- **Fire**: Manipulation of flames (Autumn Court specialty)
- **Water**: Control over water and oceans (Summer Court specialty)
- **Ice**: Creation and control of ice (Winter Court specialty)
- **Light**: Manipulation of light (Day Court, Dawn Court)
- **Darkness**: Control over shadows (Night Court specialty)
- **Wind**: Control of air currents

### Healing
- Magical restoration of wounds and ailments
- Dawn Court specialty
- Requires significant power
- Feyre has some healing ability from being Made

### Shield Creation
- Protective magical barriers
- Can protect self or others
- Mental shields protect from Daemati
- Physical shields protect from attacks

### Seer Ability
- Prophetic visions of the future
- Extremely rare
- Elain Archeron became a Seer when Made
- Visions are often cryptic

### Death Manifestation
- Nesta's unique power stolen from the Cauldron
- Ability to unmake things
- Silver fire/death flames
- Can kill even immortals

### Mating Bond
- Not a learnable magic, but a soul-deep connection
- Mates can sense each other's emotions
- Accepting the bond strengthens both partners
- Bond can be rejected (rare)

## Key Story Events

### Book 1: Amarantha's Curse (ACOTAR)
- Feyre kills a wolf (actually Andras, a Fae from the Spring Court)
- Captured by Tamlin and brought to Spring Court
- 49 years of Amarantha's captivity Under the Mountain
- All High Lords stripped of most of their powers
- Spring Court cursed with masks that couldn't be removed
- Feyre faces Three Trials to free Tamlin
- The riddle answer is "Love"
- Feyre dies and is Made by all seven High Lords
- She kills Amarantha, breaking the curse

### Book 2: Night Court Discovery (ACOMAF)
- Feyre suffers from trauma at the Spring Court
- Rhysand collects on their bargain (one week per month)
- Discovery of Velaris, the hidden City of Starlight
- Feyre trains her powers with the Inner Circle
- The mating bond is revealed between Feyre and Rhysand
- Theft of the Book of Breathings from Summer Court
- Nesta and Elain are Made by the Cauldron at Hybern
- The Wall is broken
- Feyre becomes the first High Lady in Prythian history

### Book 3: War with Hybern (ACOWAR)
- Feyre infiltrates the Spring Court as a spy
- Alliance formed with all seven courts
- Nesta's powers from the Cauldron revealed
- Elain's Seer abilities manifest
- Final war against the King of Hybern
- Rhysand sacrifices himself and is resurrected by the High Lords
- Nesta kills the King of Hybern
- Peace restored to Prythian

### The Cauldron
- Ancient magical artifact, possibly as old as the Mother herself
- Can create and destroy worlds
- Used to transform humans into High Fae
- Nesta stole power from it during her Making
- Central to Hybern's plans to break the Wall

## Important Characters from Lore

### Inner Circle of the Night Court
- **Rhysand**: High Lord of Night, most powerful High Lord in Prythian, half-Illyrian
- **Feyre Archeron**: High Lady of Night, first in Prythian history, Made with gifts from all High Lords
- **Cassian**: Illyrian general, commander of Night Court armies, born illegitimate
- **Azriel**: Shadowsinger and spymaster, scarred hands from childhood abuse
- **Mor (Morrigan)**: Wielder of Truth, escaped the Court of Nightmares, Rhysand's cousin
- **Amren**: Ancient being trapped in High Fae form, speaks many dead languages

### The Archeron Sisters
- **Feyre**: Youngest, protagonist, artist, High Lady of Night
- **Nesta**: Eldest, Made by the Cauldron, stole death from it
- **Elain**: Middle sister, Made by the Cauldron, became a Seer, mate to Lucien

### Spring Court
- **Tamlin**: High Lord of Spring, shapeshifter, Feyre's first love
- **Lucien**: Former emissary, exiled son of Beron, biological son of Helion, mate to Elain
- **Alis**: Kind servant who helped Feyre

### Antagonists
- **Amarantha**: Former general to the King of Hybern, ruled Under the Mountain for 49 years
- **King of Hybern**: Main antagonist of series, sought to destroy the Wall and enslave humans
- **Ianthe**: Treacherous priestess who betrayed Feyre's family

### Other Key Figures
- **The Bone Carver**: Ancient creature imprisoned in the Night Court's Prison
- **The Weaver**: Ancient immortal who weaves the fabric of the world, lives in the Middle
- **Bryaxis**: Living darkness imprisoned in the Prison
- **Tarquin**: Young progressive High Lord of Summer
- **Thesan**: High Lord of Dawn, known for healing
- **Helion**: High Lord of Day, spell-cleaver, Lucien's biological father
- **Kallias & Viviane**: High Lord and Lady of Winter
- **Beron**: Cruel High Lord of Autumn
- **Eris**: Heir to Autumn Court, complicated allegiances

## Locations in Detail

### Velaris (The City of Starlight)
- Hidden for millennia from enemies, even other courts
- Home to artists, musicians, merchants, and creators
- Protected by Rhysand's power and ancient spells
- Contains the Rainbow (artist and craftsman quarter)
- The House of Wind overlooks the city from a mountain peak
- Rita's bar is a famous nightlife destination
- The town house is where Feyre first stays

### Under the Mountain
- Amarantha's domain during the curse
- Massive cave system beneath a mountain
- Contains throne room, dungeons, and chambers
- Location of the three trials
- Site where Feyre was Made

### Hewn City (Court of Nightmares)
- The dark counterpart to Velaris
- Where Rhysand maintains his cruel High Lord facade
- Ancient city carved into a mountain
- Home to Mor's abusive family (Keir)
- Contains the ancient Illyrian bloodline families

### Illyrian Mountains
- Northern region of the Night Court
- Home to Illyrian war camps
- Windhaven is a major camp
- Where Cassian and Azriel were raised
- Known for harsh conditions and warrior culture

### The Prison
- Ancient prison beneath Night Court territory
- Holds the most dangerous beings in existence
- Contains Bryaxis (living darkness)
- Contained the Bone Carver
- Carved into stone with wards and spells

### The Human Lands
- Mortal realm south of the Wall
- No magic (except near the Wall)
- Impoverished after the War
- Home to the Archeron family cottage
- Now exposed to Fae after the Wall's destruction

### Hybern
- Island kingdom across the sea
- Ruled by the King of Hybern
- Did not join the Treaty after the War
- Source of the Cauldron
- Home to Amarantha's former army

## Timeline of Events

1. **The War** (500+ years before): War between humans and Fae
2. **The Treaty**: Established the Wall and peace between Fae and mortals
3. **Amarantha's Conquest**: She steals power from High Lords, begins 49-year reign
4. **Book 1 Events**: Feyre kills Andras, is captured, faces trials, Made by High Lords
5. **Book 2 Events**: Discovery of Velaris, mating bond, theft of Book, sisters Made
6. **The Wall Falls**: King of Hybern breaks the ancient barrier
7. **Book 3 Events**: War with Hybern, alliance of courts, final battle, peace

## Game Implementation Notes

The game faithfully implements:
- All seven courts with accurate descriptions and members
- "None" court option for characters without allegiance (mortals, exiles)
- Character classes based on lore (High Fae, Illyrian, Human, etc.)
- Magic system matching book abilities including:
  - Shadowsinger (Azriel's unique ability)
  - TruthTelling (Mor's power)
  - DeathManifestation (Nesta's Cauldron powers)
  - Daemati (mind reading/control)
  - Winnowing (teleportation)
  - Seer abilities (Elain)
- Quest lines following main story beats of all three books
- 81 quests covering the complete trilogy
- Accurate locations from the books
- Character transformation mechanics (human to High Fae via Cauldron)
- Court allegiance and reputation system
- Accurate character names, abilities, and backstories
- Mating bond mechanics
- Companion system with all Inner Circle members

### Lore Accuracy Features
- Under the Mountain correctly shown as neutral territory
- Human Lands and The Wall not assigned to any court
- Lucien shown without fixed court allegiance (reflects his complex status)
- Nesta's powers correctly shown as Death/Cauldron-based
- Azriel as Shadowsinger (not just darkness manipulation)
- Mor as Truth-wielder (not light manipulation)
