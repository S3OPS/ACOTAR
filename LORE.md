# ACOTAR Fantasy RPG - Lore Reference

## Overview
This document provides lore-accurate information from the ACOTAR (A Court of Thorns and Roses) series by Sarah J. Maas. This game faithfully recreates the world of Prythian.

## The World of Prythian

### The Seven Courts
Prythian is divided into seven courts, each ruled by a High Lord with unique powers:

#### 1. **Spring Court**
- **High Lord**: Tamlin
- **Powers**: Shapeshifting, nature manipulation, life force
- **Description**: Land of eternal spring with blooming gardens and temperate weather
- **Key Locations**: Spring Court Manor, Tamlin's Estate
- **Symbol**: Green and gold

#### 2. **Night Court**
- **High Lord**: Rhysand
- **Powers**: Darkness manipulation, mind reading (Daemati), winnowing, Illyrian powers
- **Description**: Split between the Court of Nightmares (Hewn City) and Velaris (City of Starlight)
- **Key Locations**: 
  - Velaris - The hidden, beautiful city
  - Hewn City - The dark court
  - Illyrian Mountains - Warrior training camps
  - House of Wind - Mountain fortress with vast library
- **Symbol**: Black and silver with stars

#### 3. **Summer Court**
- **High Lord**: Tarquin
- **Powers**: Water manipulation, ocean control
- **Description**: Coastal realm with warm waters and pristine beaches
- **Key Locations**: Adriata (capital city)
- **Symbol**: Blue and turquoise

#### 4. **Autumn Court**
- **High Lord**: Beron (later Eris)
- **Powers**: Fire manipulation, seasonal magic
- **Description**: Land of eternal autumn with forests of red, gold, and orange
- **Symbol**: Red, orange, and brown

#### 5. **Winter Court**
- **High Lord**: Kallias
- **Powers**: Ice and snow manipulation, winter magic
- **Description**: Frozen realm of eternal winter
- **Symbol**: White and ice blue

#### 6. **Dawn Court**
- **High Lord**: Thesan
- **Powers**: Healing magic, light manipulation
- **Description**: Land of perpetual sunrise, known for healers
- **Symbol**: Pink and peach

#### 7. **Day Court**
- **High Lord**: Helion
- **Powers**: Light manipulation, spell-breaking, shapeshifting
- **Description**: Sun-drenched realm, home to scholars and the greatest libraries
- **Symbol**: Gold and white

### The Wall
- Magical barrier created by the Treaty
- Separates the mortal lands from Prythian
- Can only be crossed by the Fae or with their permission

## Character Classes

### High Fae
- Immortal, powerful magical beings
- Can possess various magical abilities
- Immune to human diseases and aging
- Enhanced strength, speed, and senses

### Lesser Fae
- Magical beings of lesser power than High Fae
- Varied forms and abilities
- Include creatures like:
  - Naga
  - Boggies
  - Water wraiths
  - Various other Fae creatures

### Humans
- Mortal beings without magic (unless Made)
- Live south of the Wall
- Can be transformed into High Fae by the Cauldron

### Illyrians
- Warrior race from the Night Court mountains
- Possess powerful wings (bat-like)
- Expert fighters and warriors
- Live in war camps with strict hierarchy
- Notable members: Cassian, Azriel

### Special Creatures

#### Suriel
- Ancient Fae creatures
- Bound to tell the truth when captured
- Can be trapped with ash wood
- Possess vast knowledge

#### Attor
- Winged creature servants
- Often serve dark powers
- Swift and deadly in combat

## Magic System

### Winnowing
- Teleportation ability
- Requires great power
- Can transport self and others

### Daemati
- Mind reading and manipulation
- Rare ability
- Can enter and control minds
- Rhysand and Feyre possess this

### Shapeshifting
- Ability to change physical form
- Spring Court specialty
- Requires practice and power

### Elemental Magic
- **Fire**: Manipulation of flames (Autumn Court)
- **Water**: Control over water and oceans (Summer Court)
- **Ice**: Creation and control of ice (Winter Court)
- **Light**: Manipulation of light (Day Court)
- **Darkness**: Control over shadows (Night Court)
- **Wind**: Control of air currents

### Healing
- Magical restoration of wounds
- Dawn Court specialty
- Requires significant power

### Shield Creation
- Protective magical barriers
- Can protect self or others
- Requires concentration and power

## Key Story Events

### Amarantha's Curse (ACOTAR Book 1)
- 49 years of captivity Under the Mountain
- All High Lords except Tamlin lost their powers
- Spring Court cursed with masks
- Broken by solving riddle and acts of love

### The Cauldron
- Ancient magical artifact
- Can create and destroy
- Used to transform humans into Fae
- Central to later conflicts

### The King of Hybern
- Seeks to reclaim mortal lands
- Broke the Treaty
- Major antagonist of the series

## Important Characters from Lore

### Inner Circle of the Night Court
- **Rhysand**: High Lord
- **Cassian**: Illyrian general, warrior
- **Azriel**: Shadowsinger, spymaster
- **Mor (Morrigan)**: Truth-teller
- **Amren**: Ancient being, second-in-command

### Spring Court
- **Tamlin**: High Lord of Spring
- **Lucien**: Emissary, Autumn Court exile

### Other Key Figures
- **Feyre Archeron**: Human-turned-High-Fae, protagonist
- **Nesta & Elain Archeron**: Feyre's sisters
- **The Bone Carver**: Ancient creature imprisoned Under the Mountain
- **The Weaver**: Ancient creature in the Night Court

## Locations in Detail

### Velaris (The City of Starlight)
- Hidden for millennia from enemies
- Home to artists, musicians, and creators
- Protected by Rhysand's power
- Contains the Rainbow (artist quarter)
- The House of Wind overlooks the city

### Under the Mountain
- Amarantha's domain
- Site of the curse
- Contains throne room and dungeons
- Location of the three trials

### Hewn City
- The Court of Nightmares
- Dark counterpart to Velaris
- Where Rhysand appears as cruel ruler
- Ancient city carved into mountain

### The Human Lands
- Mortal realm south of the Wall
- No magic (except near the Wall)
- Impoverished after the War
- Home to the Archeron family cottage

## Timeline of Events

1. **The War** (500+ years before): War between humans and Fae
2. **The Treaty**: Established the Wall and peace
3. **Amarantha's Reign**: 49 years Under the Mountain
4. **Breaking the Curse**: Feyre solves riddle and breaks curse
5. **Transformation**: Feyre dies and is Made by High Lords
6. **Discovery of Velaris**: Feyre learns truth about Night Court
7. **The King of Hybern**: New war begins
